Ode to a Well-meaning Friend


And in that crisp voice, he says to write of daisies in summer – as if they themselves were idle men with undirected intellect, waving in the winds of neglect, and feeding on the rains of last month. Oh, that I too, could have such vigor. And in that crisp voice, he says to write of wonder, hope and awe – as if they were birds of prey, flapping their mighty wings above the little fields of daisies without a thought, without a pause. Oh, that I too could have such vigor. But alas, I cannot dip my feather pen in antiquated ink, nor sip that aged and vile drink, like antiquated men – like the Romance of a simpler age, that vigor has all but left my ways. And in that crisp voice, he says, to write about the daisies in summer…
